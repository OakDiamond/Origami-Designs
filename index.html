Interesting Facts about Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts about Cicada

  • There are around 3,000 cicada species
  • Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.
  • Cicadas can live a relatively long time: 4 to 17 years, depending on whether they are annual or periodical cicadas. The periodical cicadas live the longest.

Interesting Facts about Panda

  • High in dense bamboo forests in the misty, rainy mountains of southwestern China lives one of the world's rarest mammals: the giant panda, also called the panda.
  • Pandas eat almost nothing but bamboo shoots and leaves. Occasionally they eat other vegetation, fish, or small animals, but bamboo accounts for 99 percent of their diets.The reason: They digest only about a fifth of what they eat. Overall, bamboo is not very nutritious. To stay healthy, they have to eat a lot—up to 15 percent of their body weight in 12 hours—so they eat fast.
  • Pandas eat fast, they eat a lot, and they spend about 12 hours a day doing it.

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons have lived alongside man for thousands of years with the first images of pigeons being found by archaeologists in Mesopotamia (modern Iraq) and dating back to 3000BC.
  • In modern times the pigeon has been used to great effect during wartime. In both the First and Second World Wars the pigeon saved hundreds of thousands of human lives by carrying messages across enemy lines.
  • The earliest large-scale communication network using pigeons as messengers was established in Syria and Persia around the 5th century BC.

Interesting Facts about Teddy

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
